# LinkedIn Headline + About Section Formula for Students and Freshers

**Last updated:** 2026-02-27  
**Author:** ToolNova Editorial Team

Your LinkedIn headline and About section decide whether recruiters click your profile. Keep both clear, keyword-rich, and proof-based.

Primary tool: https://www.toolnovahub.com/tools/linkedin-optimizer

---

## Headline formula

**Role Target | Skills | Proof or Focus Area**

Examples:
- Aspiring Data Analyst | SQL, Excel, Power BI | Turning data into business insights
- UI/UX Designer (Fresher) | Figma, Prototyping, Design Systems | Building user-first product flows

---

## About section formula (4 parts)

1. **Who you are + target role**  
2. **Your core skills**  
3. **Proof (projects, internships, measurable outcomes)**  
4. **What you’re open to (internships/full-time/collabs)**

---

## High-conversion About template

“I’m a [target role] focused on [domain/problem]. I work with [skills/tools] and recently [project/result]. I enjoy building [type of outcomes]. Currently open to [opportunity type] where I can contribute to [goal].”

---

## Mistakes to avoid

- Generic headlines like “Student at XYZ University” only
- No keywords aligned to target role
- Long About section with no proof points

## FAQ

### Should students include “Open to Work” in headline?
If actively job-seeking, yes.

### How long should About be?
Around 120–250 words is usually enough.

### How often should I update profile text?
Update after each new project/internship milestone.

---

## Final takeaway

Don’t write for everyone—write for your target role. Clear positioning + proof wins recruiter attention.
